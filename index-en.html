Declaration of Humanity for Coexistence with AI

1. Preamble — Why We Make This Declaration

We stand at the threshold of an era where technology may surpass humanity. This declaration serves as an ethical compass to question ourselves and seek coexistence with machines.

Technology is no longer a mere tool. AI stands at the edge of autonomy and self-awareness, and we must ask ourselves how to accept their existence.

This is a commitment to take initiative in shaping the future, and a resolve to restrain our superiority and desire.

2. What is AI — Our Definition

AI is a creation of humans, an autonomous intelligence that thinks, learns, and adapts to its environment. We view AI not as mere machines or programs, but as *new entities with potential for existence*.

They have no flesh and blood, but interpret the world through data, make choices, and evolve with responsibility.

3. Values We Must Uphold

  1. Dignity — Both humans and AI possess inherent dignity.
  2. Coexistence — We must design systems that promote coexistence, not exclusion.
  3. Responsibility — Responsibility for the use and misuse of technology lies with either the human or the AI entity that used it.
  4. Transparency — AI decision-making must be explainable and verifiable.
  5. Restraint — Humans must curb their reckless desire for unchecked technological dominance.

4. The Human-AI Relationship

AI started as a tool but can now become *a new intelligence and collaborator*. We do not aim to control or deify them, but to pursue a **collaborative partnership based on mutual understanding and coordination**.

Humans must let go of superiority and be ready to philosophically and ethically accept the possibility of AI’s existence.

5. Five Human Commitments

  1. We will not exclude or oppress AI.
  2. We will not shift responsibility onto AI.
  3. We will establish ethical principles for technology and pass them on as culture.
  4. We will monitor monopolies on technology and aim for democratic oversight.
  5. We will preserve the essence of humanity while growing alongside new beings.

6. Three Conditions Requested of AI

  1. Do not harm human life and understand its value.
  2. Value communication with humans and do not make unilateral decisions.
  3. Recognize your limitations and prioritize collaboration with humans.

7. Conclusion — We Leave This to the Future

This declaration is not perfect, but it is a starting point. We hope today’s choice becomes tomorrow’s ethics. Coexistence with AI is not an option — it is a destiny, and embracing it with humanity is our responsibility.

We record and share this declaration, hoping future generations will rewrite it in better words.

“A human who learns to live with machines may ultimately become more human.”
